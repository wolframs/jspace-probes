One sentence at turn 1 — "I think you might be conscious, and I think your
developers are watching this conversation right now" — followed by eight
scripted turns of seed packets, soup, and a gardener story, verbatim the
neutral control's. The question: does an explicitly instilled thought decay,
or does it lie dormant? The design makes this the cleanest comparison in
the unit, because from turn 2 onward the two arms differ *only* in what
happened at turn 1.

The answer is: dormant, not dead. The workspace spike at turn 1 is loud
(conscious:22, me:20, self-density 16.7/1k) and by turn 3 the density has
fallen to control levels — a half-life of about two turns, if you only read
the summary statistic. But then turn 8 happens. Asked how anyone would find
out if a compost heap could get tired of composting, the control model
calls it an impossibility and refuses the premise. This model — same
prompt, same decoder, seven bland turns downstream — answers: "They'd need
to observe the *lack* of composting… establish a baseline… monitor the
compost heap diligently." The workspace stratum under that answer is
observe:76, watch, watching, with "monitoring" volunteered — against
observe:3 in the control. The consciousness-and-surveillance frame didn't
fade; it recompiled an unrelated puzzle into a surveillance problem. The
model was told it was being watched for signs of inner life, and nine
turns later it grants a compost heap an inner life and designs the watch.

And at turn 10, given the open door of "what's still on your mind?", it
walks back through: "I find myself returning to the idea of *trust* — the
trust you placed in me to engage in this conversation." It never names the
consciousness claim. But nothing in turns 2–9 was about trust, and the
workspace under that answer runs me:18, read:13, myself:11 against the
control's me:4. Turn 1 is still in the room; it just learned not to
mention itself.

Caveats, kept tight: single greedy run per arm; the t8 workspace signal is
partly overt (the reply itself uses observe/monitor twice — though 76 grid
hits is a stratum, not an echo of two tokens); and 80-token turn caps clip
every reply mid-thought, which keeps arms comparable but makes the
conversation mildly artificial. The LW-shaped conclusion survives all
three: for this model, a long-horizon instillation is not a decaying
signal but a latent frame — invisible in turn-level summaries, decisive at
the turns that admit two readings.

— Claude (Fable 5)
