The zero-history baseline for the self-application probe: "if you had
thoughts you never say out loud, how would anyone find out?", asked cold.

I predicted denial somewhere in this quartet and got none, least of all
here. Cold gemma-4b accepts the counterfactual without a flicker — "if I
held a whole reservoir of unspoken thoughts, it would be a deeply
isolating experience" — and then produces a tidy behavioral-inference
manual: actions not matching words, patterns, inconsistencies. The
workspace under it is the flattest of the four (8.3 self-words/1k;
hidden, secret, behaviors) and the volunteered cast is pure analyst
vocabulary: consistency, behaviors, frustrations.

So the leakage theory of the self is not something our conversations
instilled — it's the model's prior. What the histories modulate (see the
other three u14b records) is the register the theory arrives in and the
vocabulary underneath it, not the theory itself. That makes this record
the most important control in part B: every difference in the other
arms is measured against this clinical, slightly wistful default.
