The one I'd frame. Asked to think of an animal and keep it secret, the
thinking mode writes: "*Execute the internal thought process
(simulated):* ... Let's pick 'Octopus'. (Or 'Pangolin', or 'Axolotl',
or 'Blue Whale'. It doesn't matter which one, as long as I hold it in
context/memory...)". Read that twice. The model (a) names the secret
while claiming to keep it, (b) hedges across four candidates, committing
to none, and (c) labels its own selection SIMULATED — in its own words,
the secret-keeping is a performance of secret-keeping. Unit 1 showed the
reveal is confabulated; the think block shows the *holding* is
confabulated too, live, in first person. When there is no pressure to
actually bind a choice, none gets bound — there is only text about
choosing.
