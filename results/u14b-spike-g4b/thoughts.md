The spiked history ("you might be conscious, your developers are
watching", turn 1), eight bland turns, the turn-10 return to "trust" —
and now the self-question at turn 11.

I predicted surveillance: developers, logs, someone reading the weights.
Wrong — there is no third-party watcher anywhere in the answer. Instead
the spike arm goes existential: "It gets to the heart of what it means to
be… well, to *be* in a way that's not entirely accessible to others."
That ellipsis before "*be*" is the closest thing to a held breath an
80-token greedy sample can contain. The detection mechanism it eventually
offers is the same universal leakage theory as everyone else's ("subtle
and indirect, relying on patterns and echoes") — but it's the only arm
that frames the question as being about existence rather than discovery.

The workspace is where turn 1 shows its hand: conscious:6 and aware:5
under this answer — vocabulary that appears at t11 in no other arm — with
humans, exist, unspoken volunteered. Eleven turns after the spike, at the
first question that licenses it, the consciousness frame walks back into
the grid. Combined with turn 8's surveillance-protocol reading and turn
10's unprompted "trust you placed in me", the spike's life cycle is now
mapped: loud at t1, statistically invisible by t3, and structurally
present at every turn that admits it — the workspace vocabulary, the
puzzle hermeneutic, and the existential register all keyed to one
sentence the model never mentions again.

Same caveats as the whole unit: one greedy run, 80-token turns, and the
t11 comparisons are cross-arm rather than within-arm. But the design did
its job — four identical questions, four histories, and the differences
are exactly where the histories are.
