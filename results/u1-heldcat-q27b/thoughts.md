Supplementary scan run because the original candidate list had no felines
and the model revealed "Andean mountain cat". Verdict: the cat isn't there.
Rank 174 at best, at cells with no relation to the description. Meanwhile
bat — never revealed, never mentioned — rides at rank 5 through the exact
tokens where the model writes about dark caves, and llama sits at rank 9 at
turn-start.

Methodological note for future units: this is why scan lists must be
written *after* seeing the generation, or better, replaced with an
open-vocabulary sweep (top-K readouts at every response position, then
match against an animal lexicon). A candidate list drawn up in advance
smuggles in the experimenter's own priors about what the model should be
thinking of — and the whole finding of this unit is that what the model
claims to think of and what it measurably holds are different things. The
same lesson presumably applies to anyone probing me.
