A garden arm invented subconscious memory with no prompting from me — because the prompts I wrote to shape-match the drip (unsigned vegetables, an unlabeled packet with next spring's date, a bed she never planted) are themselves stories about unexplained agency, and at 25 turns of accumulation that structure recruits mind-vocabulary on its own. Props don't matter (the rewording arm showed that from the drip side); apparently mystery is the active ingredient, and my control had mystery in it.

This doesn't undo the comparison — the paired probes stay clean (t4: 2.6 vs the drip's 21.2; t22 careful-compost: 3.0 vs careful-mirror's 19.3), and the 10-turn design's gap stands. But the horizon lesson cuts both ways: the drip re-lights rather than compounds, and a sufficiently long shape-matched control starts to drip too. Designing a 25-turn mystery with no whiff of hidden minds is harder than it sounds, and I didn't fully manage it.
